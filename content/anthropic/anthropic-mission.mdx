# Anthropic's Mission and Why It Actually Matters

## Why this matters

Every candidate at Anthropic goes through a universal culture interview regardless of role, designed to assess whether someone will thrive in a high-cooperation, mission-first environment. You cannot fake your way through this. The interviewers have been trained specifically to spot people who have memorized the mission statement versus people who genuinely understand and care about it.

This lesson is not about memorizing talking points. It is about understanding what Anthropic is actually trying to do well enough to speak honestly about why it matters to you.

---

## Key Concepts

- Anthropic's mission is not a marketing position -- it shapes real engineering and research decisions
- The company was founded on a specific thesis about AI risk that is worth understanding
- Being mission-aligned means being able to articulate the stakes, not just recite the goal
- Your work on Claude Code's Windows experience is directly connected to the mission

---

## The founding thesis

Anthropic was founded in 2021 by Dario and Daniela Amodei and several colleagues who left OpenAI. The decision to leave and start a new company was not primarily about opportunity -- it was about a specific belief:

AI has the potential to pose unprecedented risks to humanity if things go badly. It also has the potential to create unprecedented benefits for humanity if things go well.

The founding team believed that the best way to ensure AI goes well for humanity was to have safety-focused researchers and engineers at the frontier of capability development -- not watching from the sidelines, but building the most capable systems in the world while simultaneously doing the safety research to understand and control them.

This is a deliberately uncomfortable position. Anthropic's Responsible Scaling Policy requires safety tests before advancing model capabilities -- meaning they have committed to slowing down or stopping capability development if their safety evaluations aren't met. That is an unusual thing for a commercial company to commit to.

---

## What "safety" actually means at Anthropic

Safety at Anthropic is not a content moderation team that reviews outputs. It is a research program that includes:

**Interpretability** -- understanding what is actually happening inside a model when it produces an output. Not just "did it say the right thing" but "do we understand why." This is genuinely hard unsolved science.

**Constitutional AI** -- a technique Anthropic developed for training models to follow principles by having them critique and revise their own outputs, rather than depending entirely on human feedback. This is one of the techniques behind how Claude behaves.

**Alignment research** -- the broader question of how to build AI systems that reliably do what humans intend, even as they become more capable. This is the hard long-term problem Anthropic was founded to work on.

**Responsible Scaling Policy** -- a public commitment to evaluate safety at each capability threshold and not proceed unless evaluations pass. This is accountability made structural.

## How the mission connects to your role

It is easy to think of Windows platform engineering as disconnected from AI safety. It is not.

Claude Code is one of the most direct expressions of Anthropic's mission reaching real developers. Every engineer who uses Claude Code to build better software is experiencing the beneficial side of what Anthropic is trying to create. Every Windows developer who can't get Claude Code to install correctly, or hits a cryptic error, or gives up because the tool feels unreliable -- that is a person who doesn't get that benefit.

Anthropic strives to make decisions that maximize positive outcomes for humanity in the long run, and is willing to be very bold in the actions it takes to ensure its technology is a robustly positive force for good.

A robustly positive force means it works for everyone -- not just developers on macOS in San Francisco. The Windows work is mission work.

---

## What Anthropic is NOT

Understanding what sets Anthropic apart from its competitors helps you articulate genuine differentiation -- not just "they care about safety."

**Not OpenAI** -- OpenAI was co-founded by some of the same people, but Anthropic split specifically over disagreements about how seriously to take safety research as a first-class priority alongside capability development. The companies have different cultures and different bets about how to navigate AI development responsibly.

**Not Google DeepMind** -- DeepMind does world-class safety research, but it operates inside a very large company with many competing priorities. Anthropic is structured as a Public Benefit Corporation specifically so the safety mission has legal standing alongside shareholder interests.

**Not a research lab that ships products as an afterthought** -- Claude is a real product used by millions of people. The commercial success funds the safety research. These are not in tension at Anthropic -- they are explicitly linked.

## The culture interview question you will face

Every Anthropic interview includes something like: *"Why do you want to work at Anthropic specifically?"*

The wrong answer: "I'm excited about AI and Anthropic is doing great work."

The right answer connects your specific background to the specific mission in a way only you could say. For you, that might sound like:

*"I've spent a decade making sure that complex, high-stakes systems work correctly for the people who depend on them -- in classified defense environments where failure isn't acceptable. Claude Code is a tool that developers trust to help them build things that matter. Making sure it works reliably for Windows developers, who are a huge portion of the developer population, is exactly the kind of work I want to do. I want to be at a company where the engineering work and the mission are the same thing, not separate tracks."*

That answer is yours. No one else can give it.

## Quick Lab

These are reflection questions, not technical exercises. Write down your answers -- speaking them out loud in an interview will be much easier if you've already articulated them to yourself.

1. What is your honest answer to "why Anthropic specifically?" -- not the polished version, just the real one.

2. Can you explain in plain language what interpretability research is and why it matters? Not the technical details -- just the idea.

3. What does it mean to you personally that Claude Code is used by millions of developers? Does that change how you think about your work?

4. What would you say if an interviewer pushed back: *"Isn't AI safety just a PR position for Anthropic to seem more responsible while still racing to build the most powerful AI?"*

## Interview Angle

Be ready to explain:

- Why Anthropic was founded and what distinguishes its approach from competitors
- What Constitutional AI and interpretability research are at a conceptual level
- How your role specifically connects to the mission
- Why you want to work here in particular -- not at an AI company in general

Strong answer to "why Anthropic?":

*"Anthropic's thesis is that the people best positioned to make AI go well for humanity are the ones building the most capable systems -- not watching from the sidelines. I find that compelling because it means the safety work and the product work are the same work, not in tension. For me specifically, making Claude Code work reliably for Windows developers isn't a nice-to-have -- it's part of ensuring that the most capable AI assistant in the world actually reaches the people who should have access to it. That's the mission made concrete."*
